## Who I Am

I'm a Site Reliability Engineer at **Snapp** (Iran's leading ride-hailing platform) working on large-scale distributed systems. I'm passionate about infrastructure reliability, observability, and building systems that work.

Beyond my day job, I'm the creator of **Goalixa** — a productivity platform I've been developing to deepen my understanding of modern software architecture, from microservices to GitOps deployments.

## What I Do

### At Snapp {#section-about-work}

As an SRE, I work on:
- **OpenShift** clusters for containerized workloads
- **Ceph** storage infrastructure
- Complex alerting rules for system reliability
- Message queue technologies (RabbitMQ, Kafka, NATS)
- Ensuring reliability for critical services
- Large-scale distributed systems

### Personal Projects {#section-about-projects}

**Goalixa** is my learning playground where I experiment with:
- Complete monitoring stacks (Prometheus, Grafana, Alertmanager)
- Microservices architecture
- API Gateway patterns (BFF)
- Progressive Web Apps (PWA)
- GitOps with ArgoCD
- Event-driven architecture

> **Why the complex architecture?** Goalixa is where I practice platform engineering and DevOps concepts hands-on. It might seem overengineered for a personal project, but learning is the main goal.

## Philosophy & Approach

### Learning in Public {#section-about-philosophy}

I believe in sharing knowledge through experience. Whether it's documenting incident reports, writing about architecture decisions, or sharing progress recaps — I learn by doing and documenting.

### Experimentation Over Perfection

Goalixa isn't perfect, and that's the point. It's a space where I can try new technologies, make mistakes, build patterns that I can apply at work, and explore solutions for educational purposes.
